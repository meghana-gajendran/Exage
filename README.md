# ExAge

ExAge helps learners discover what they don't know they don't know.

Most learning tools answer questions.

ExAge focuses on uncovering hidden gaps in understanding and helping learners discover the next questions worth asking.

## Why ExAge Exists

Many learners can:

- pass exams
- solve assignments
- recall definitions

while still lacking a complete understanding of how a system works.

The problem is that learners often don't know which questions they should ask next.

ExAge helps surface those hidden questions.

## Start With Your Goal

Before exploring a topic, ExAge asks:

Why are you learning this?

Examples:

- Pass an exam
- Teach a class
- Build a product
- Prepare for interviews
- Explore out of curiosity

Your learning objective influences:

- what gaps matter
- what questions are generated
- what explanations are prioritized

## Example

Topic: Kubernetes

Learner says:

"I understand pods, deployments and scaling."

ExAge identifies:

- missing mental models
- possible misconceptions
- unexplored questions
- system-level gaps

Example questions:

- What happens after kubectl apply?
- How does Kubernetes recover from node failures?
- Why are pods ephemeral?
- What component decides where a pod runs?

## Core Capabilities

### Active Probing

Uses targeted questions to expose hidden gaps in understanding.

### Mental Model Analysis

Analyzes a learner's explanation and identifies missing concepts and assumptions.

### Systems Visualization

Connects isolated concepts into an end-to-end flow.

### Curiosity Paths

Suggests meaningful directions for deeper exploration.

## Vision

Move learning from:

"Can I answer this question?"

to

"What questions am I not asking yet?"
